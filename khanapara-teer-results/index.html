History of Khanapara Teer Game

The state government didn't legalize the Khanapara Teer game until the middle of the 1980s, and Teer betting was also outlawed at that time. But in 1982, the state government not only approved it but also saw that it was a reliable source of money. The Assam Assembly enacted the measure known as the Assam Regulation of the Game of Arrow Shooting and the Selling of Teer Tickets on March 23, 2018, to further regulate arrow shooting games and Teer ticket sales. Tickets are sold at authorized Teer counters in Meghalaya from 10 AM to 3:30 PM every day, excluding Sundays.

How is the Khanapara Teer game played?

Except on Sundays, Khanapara Teer is played every day of the week. The goal of the Khanapara dream Teer game is to predict the final two digits of the total number of Tears that struck the target. We may use the number 860 as an example. The player who correctly predicted "60" as the final two digits won the game if 860 arrows struck the target.
